# Rails — parallel progression

> A **rail** is a self-contained progression track that runs *alongside* the main 1→300 climb. The main rail is levels. The side rails are professions, renown, PvP, delves, exploration, housing, collections. You're always on the main rail *and* free to hop a side rail — so a one-year journey is **never one grind**. → [pillars](../00-design-pillars.md) #5, #8, #9

## Why rails exist
A year to cap ([progression](README.md)) is a gift only if the year has texture. WoW's failure mode was the *single mandatory treadmill*: the daily/weekly lockout was the one true path to power, so play became a chore-list and a missed day was a loss ([pillars](../00-design-pillars.md) #5). Rails fix this structurally — **many tracks, all granting real permanent progress**, none mandatory. Log in and ask "what do I feel like today?", not "what do I *have* to do today?".

Think of a train yard: the **main line** (levels) always goes forward, but you can switch onto a parallel line whenever you like, ride it as far as you want, and switch back. Every line reaches a real destination.

## The rails
| Rail | What you ride it for | Grants (permanent, account-wide unless noted) |
|---|---|---|
| **Levels** (main) | The world story, zones, the [Ages](ages.md) | Levels, ability & talent unlocks ([talents](../classes/talents-and-hero-trees.md)) |
| **Renown** | Faction & world-power reputations, per Age | Small stat/utility bonuses, cosmetics, unlock keys — **effort-metered, not calendar-gated** ([pillars](../00-design-pillars.md) #8) |
| **Professions** | Crafting & gathering mastery | Recipes, quality tiers, gear you can *make* to bridge raid tiers ([professions](../world-systems/professions.md), [itemization](../itemization.md)) |
| **PvP ranks** | Battlegrounds, arenas, world PvP | Rank rewards, PvP gear ladder, prestige ([pvp](../endgame/pvp.md)) |
| **Delve mastery** | Solo/small-group scalable dives | Delve tiers, targetable gear, companion progression ([delves](../endgame/delves.md)) |
| **Exploration** | Seeing the world; the [living world](../world-systems/living-world.md) | Map completion, skyriding glyphs, discovery rewards, lore |
| **Housing** | Your place in the world | Plot upgrades, decor, neighborhood standing ([housing](../world-systems/housing.md)) |
| **Collections** | Mounts, appearances, companions, titles | The [warband](#the-warband-account-wide) wardrobe — pure prestige, never power-gated by FOMO ([pillars](../00-design-pillars.md) #11) |

## Rules of the rails (what keeps them pro-player)
- **All rails grant permanent, additive progress** — never borrowed power ([pillars](../00-design-pillars.md) #1).
- **No rail is mandatory.** You can cap raid-ready on the main + one rail; the others are breadth, not tax.
- **Effort-metered, not time-metered.** A rail advances when you *do* the thing, not when the daily reset permits it. Catch-up is generous.
- **Rails are account-wide** where it doesn't unbalance an era — see the warband, below.
- **Power from rails is small and horizontal.** Renown/profession bonuses are flavor and utility, not a second gear-score you *must* max ([pillars](../00-design-pillars.md) #6).

## The warband (account-wide)
The account, not the character, is the unit of collection. Appearances, mounts, titles, exploration, most renown, and shared currencies/knowledge live on the **warband** and are earned once ([pillars](../00-design-pillars.md) #9). This kills the alt-hostility that made every second character re-grind the same unlocks. Power that *would* unbalance a frozen [era](era-servers.md) stays character-bound; cosmetics and knowledge travel.

## Rails × era servers
On an [era server](era-servers.md), the rails available are those of its unlocked [Ages](ages.md) — an 80-era world has the renown, professions, and PvP of Ages I–III, complete and toppable. Rails are part of what makes a frozen era a *whole* game, not a stripped one.

## Rules
- **Many tracks, none mandatory** — the antidote to the single treadmill.
- **All rail progress is permanent & additive**, mostly account-wide (warband).
- **Effort-metered, not calendar-gated.** Generous catch-up.
- **Rail power is small & horizontal** — breadth and flavor, never a rival gear-score.
