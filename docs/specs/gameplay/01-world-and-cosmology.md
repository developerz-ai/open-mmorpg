# 01 — World & Cosmology

> The setting exists to give the [Ages](progression/ages.md) a spine and the [factions](factions/README.md) a reason. It is deliberately archetypal — a living world, a slumbering power, two peoples, six forces in tension — so that 25 Ages of content have somewhere to go. All names original ([legal](../../initial-idea/01-legal-and-licensing.md)).

## Auralon — the living world
**Auralon** is a world that is itself alive: at its core sleeps the **Emberheart**, a nascent world-soul — a god not yet born. Every cosmic power in the setting wants to claim, wake, corrupt, or extinguish it. Mortals live on the surface, mostly unaware they are standing on a slowly-waking titan of a soul. The **Emberheart Saga** — the arc of that waking — is the through-line of the endgame ([raids](endgame/raids.md)): each Age, another force makes its move.

Reimagines: Azeroth-as-a-world-soul and the Worldsoul Saga — the *concept* of a living planet at the center of a cosmic tug-of-war. Original expression throughout.

## The six cosmic forces
Reality is a standing wave of six forces in eternal tension. Every class, faction, and endgame threat draws from one or more. Balance, not victory, is the natural state — a force *winning* is always a catastrophe.

| Force | Domain | Feels like | Class/threat expression |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lattice** | Order, structure, arcane, the makers | Cold geometry, ley-lines, constructs | Arcanists; the world's titan-machinery; rigid-order antagonists |
| **Rift** | Chaos, ruin, invasion, unmaking | Fire, teeth, wrongness from outside | Riftblades, Occultists; the demonic incursions ([ages](progression/ages.md)) |
| **Bloom** | Life, growth, the wild, cycles | Overgrowth, fecundity, spring | Wilders, Wardens; nature-overrun threats |
| **Hollow** | Death, decay, the after, entropy | Grey, still, patient | Revenants, Oracles(Umbra); the undead reclamation |
| **Dawn** | Radiance, hope, judgment, healing | Warm gold, resolve | Templars, Oracles(Radiance); zealot-order antagonists |
| **Deep** | Void, madness, the unknowable | Whispers, tentacular dark | Occultists, Oracles(Umbra); the whispering endgame threat |

**Design note:** the forces map onto WoW's six-fold cosmology (Order/Fel/Life/Death/Light/Void) because that hexagram is *good design* — it gives every class a philosophical home and every villain a legible motive. We keep the structure, own the expression, and — the reversal — refuse the "Light and Void are secretly the real final bosses" retcon whiplash. Here the Saga's antagonist is always **imbalance**, and any force can be the aggressor in a given Age.

## Geography (data-driven, grows with the Ages)
- Two home continents anchor the factions: **the Concord's** dawnlit heartlands and **the Pact's** untamed frontier. Neutral and contested zones between.
- Each Age opens new geography — a new landmass, a shattered moon, a plane-touched region, the world's interior as the Emberheart stirs. Zones are [content assets](../game-server/content-scripting/README.md), streamed seamlessly ([world-model](../game-server/world-model/README.md)); no loading screens by design ([feature-bar](../../initial-idea/08-feature-bar.md)).
- The world is **one seamless space per shard-set**, autoscaled ([sharding](../game-server/sharding/README.md)) — not a corridor of instanced flatlands.

## The two peoples (summary — full lore in [factions](factions/README.md))
- **The Aurelian Concord** — those who *build*: cities, law, the arcane academy, the dawn-faith. Order-and-Dawn leaning but pluralist; a coalition, not an empire.
- **The Wildreach Pact** — those who *endure*: tribes, ancestors, the primal spirits, the exiled and the reclaimed. Life-and-Hollow leaning; a confederation bound by freedom and survival.

Neither is "the good guys." The reversal ([pillars](00-design-pillars.md) #4): their conflict is real and ideological, but it is **not** a mandatory war — against a waking-world threat they cooperate, and players cross the line freely.

## Rules
- **Lore is data** — zones, factions, the Saga beats are content, not code. New Age = new lore asset, no recompile.
- **No force is "the good one."** Any Age's antagonist is a force out of balance; heroes restore tension, they don't win the cosmology.
- **The Emberheart is never "used up."** It is the setting's renewable engine — every Age is another chapter of its waking, so content can run to Age XXV and beyond.
